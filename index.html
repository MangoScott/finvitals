01

H.R. 1 is happening now

H.R. 1 just passed. It contains the largest proposed cuts to SNAP in history, expands work requirements to age 64, and imposes new Medicaid community engagement requirements. Millions of Americans are about to lose food and health benefits. Most of them don't know it yet.

This isn't abstract. In Wolfe County, Kentucky — where we've done two years of field research with UVA — 135,000 people are at risk of losing SNAP. In Chicago, 50% of eligible elderly aren't even receiving SNAP benefits now, and the new requirements will push more off. In Atlanta's majority-Black neighborhoods, only 38% of SNAP retailers carry fresh produce. These communities are already stretched thin. H.R. 1 is about to snap them.

the numbers are real people

63% of Americans can't cover a $500 emergency. Every dollar of SNAP benefits returns $1.54 in GDP. When people lose benefits, they don't just go hungry — they skip medications, miss rent, and end up in emergency rooms. The programs exist. The funding exists. But the people who need them most are the last to find out they're losing them.

02

what we already built

FinMango didn't start building when the crisis hit. The infrastructure has been running for years. FinVitals is the AI that makes it act.

  • BarometerTracks financial stress across all 50 states daily — anxiety, food insecurity, housing stress, affordability — from BLS, Census, HUD, FRED, and Google Trends. It can see the crisis forming right now.
  • Food Assistance CalculatorCross-program eligibility screening. One conversation checks SNAP, WIC, school meals, food pantries, and utility assistance. Already live, already serving real people.
  • Food Desert AnalyzerMaps grocery access, fresh food availability, and transportation barriers by zip code. Shows exactly where food access breaks down.
  • Affordability LabInteractive housing affordability analysis — rent burden, income-to-housing ratios, cost-of-living pressure across U.S. communities.

The seeing part works. The connecting part works. What's missing is the AI layer between them — the part that takes a Barometer signal in Wolfe County and automatically reaches every affected household with the specific programs they still qualify for. That's FinVitals.

03

see it. stop it.

FinVitals is the AI layer between seeing a crisis and stopping it. Two steps. That's it.

step one

see who's at risk

AI analyzes Barometer signals alongside H.R. 1 policy changes and identifies which communities and households are about to lose benefits. Not "135,000 Kentuckians" as a statistic — but which families, in which zip codes, losing which programs, by what deadline. A spike in financial anxiety in Wolfe County plus SNAP work requirement expansion to age 64 doesn't just show up on a dashboard. It triggers action.

step two

reach them first

Proactively connect at-risk households with the specific programs they still qualify for — before the deadline hits. The Food Assistance Calculator and Food Desert Analyzer stop being tools people have to find and become tools that find people. One conversation. Every alternative. Personalized to their situation, location, and timeline. The goal: nobody loses benefits they could have kept because nobody told them in time.

04

the tool states need right now

State SNAP and Medicaid directors are overwhelmed by H.R. 1 implementation. They have to notify affected households. They don't have the tools to do it.

Every state implementing H.R. 1 changes faces the same problem: they know the new rules are coming, they know people will lose benefits, and they have no scalable way to identify who's affected and reach them in time. Caseworker teams are already stretched. Legacy systems weren't built for proactive outreach. The clock is ticking.

FinVitals gives them what they've never had:

real-time visibility

See financial stress across your state as it's happening — not six months later in an annual report

predictive targeting

Know exactly which households are at risk of losing benefits under H.R. 1 — and what alternatives exist for each one

automatic outreach

Reach affected people with the specific programs they qualify for — without overwhelming caseworker teams

ready to deploy

Runs through existing infrastructure: state health departments, SNAP offices, 211 centers, public libraries. Zero new systems to build

05

about FinMango

FinMango is a 501(c)(3) I founded in 2017 with one idea: financial health is a right, not a privilege. We've reached over 1 million people across 100+ countries. Our Google Health partnership was published in Nature. We've spent two years doing field research with the University of Virginia across five communities — including the ones H.R. 1 will hit hardest.

We work with Google Health, the WHO, the World Bank, and the IMF. Everything we build is open source. The Barometer, the Food Assistance Calculator, the Food Desert Analyzer — they're all live, all free, all running. FinVitals doesn't start from scratch. It connects the pieces we've already built with the AI layer the moment demands.
