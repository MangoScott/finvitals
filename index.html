01

H.R. 1 is happening now

H.R. 1 just passed. It contains the largest proposed cuts to SNAP in history. Work requirements expanding to age 64. New Medicaid community engagement mandates. Millions of people are about to lose their food and health benefits — and most of them have no idea.

We know because we've been in these communities. In Wolfe County, Kentucky — where we spent two years doing field research with UVA — 135,000 people are at risk of losing SNAP. In Chicago, half of eligible seniors don't receive SNAP now; the new rules will push even more out. In Atlanta's majority-Black neighborhoods, only 38% of SNAP retailers carry fresh produce. These are communities already running on fumes. H.R. 1 is about to pull the plug.

these aren't statistics

63% of Americans can't cover a $500 emergency. Every dollar of SNAP returns $1.54 to the economy. When someone loses benefits, they don't just go hungry — they skip their meds, fall behind on rent, end up in the ER. The programs exist. The money is there. But the people who need help the most are always the last to hear they're losing it.

02

what we already built

We didn't start building when the crisis hit. This infrastructure has been running for years. FinVitals is the AI that finally makes it act.

  • BarometerTracks financial stress across all 50 states daily — anxiety, food insecurity, housing stress, affordability — from BLS, Census, HUD, FRED, and Google Trends. It can see the crisis forming right now.
  • Food Assistance CalculatorCross-program eligibility screening. One conversation checks SNAP, WIC, school meals, food pantries, and utility assistance. Already live, already serving real people.
  • Food Desert AnalyzerMaps grocery access, fresh food availability, and transportation barriers by zip code. Shows exactly where food access breaks down.
  • Affordability LabInteractive housing affordability analysis — rent burden, income-to-housing ratios, cost-of-living pressure across U.S. communities.

So the seeing works. The connecting works. What's missing is the thing in the middle — the part that takes a Barometer signal in Wolfe County and automatically reaches every affected household with the programs they still qualify for. That's what FinVitals is.

03

see it. stop it.

The gap isn't data. It's not tools. It's the space between knowing something bad is coming and actually reaching the people it's going to hit. FinVitals closes that gap. Two steps.

step one

see who's at risk

The Barometer is already tracking financial stress in real time. Layer in H.R. 1 policy changes and you can see exactly who's about to lose what. Not "135,000 Kentuckians" — which families, which zip codes, which programs, by when. A spike in financial anxiety in Wolfe County plus SNAP work requirements expanding to age 64 doesn't just sit on a dashboard. It triggers the next step.

step two

reach them first

Once you know who's at risk, reach them — with the exact programs they still qualify for, before the deadline. The Food Assistance Calculator and Food Desert Analyzer stop being things people have to find and start finding people. One conversation. Every alternative. Personalized to where they live and what they're about to lose. The goal is simple: nobody falls off a cliff because nobody told them there was a bridge.

04

the tool states need right now

Talk to any state SNAP or Medicaid director right now. They're drowning. H.R. 1 requires them to notify affected households — but they don't have anything close to the tools they'd need to actually do it.

Every state faces the same thing: they know the rules are changing, they know people will fall off, and they have no way to figure out who or reach them before it happens. Caseworkers are already buried. The legacy systems weren't built for this. Nobody's is.

FinVitals gives them what they've never had:

real-time visibility

See financial stress across your state as it's happening — not six months later in an annual report

predictive targeting

Know exactly which households are at risk of losing benefits under H.R. 1 — and what alternatives exist for each one

automatic outreach

Reach affected people with the specific programs they qualify for — without overwhelming caseworker teams

ready to deploy

Runs through existing infrastructure: state health departments, SNAP offices, 211 centers, public libraries. Zero new systems to build

05

about FinMango

I started FinMango in 2017 as a 501(c)(3) with one belief: financial health is a right, not a privilege. What started as a classroom project at Kent State has reached over 1 million people across 100+ countries. We published with Google Health in Nature. We spent two years in the field with UVA across five communities — including the ones H.R. 1 is about to hit hardest.

Our partners include Google Health, the WHO, the World Bank, and the IMF. Everything we build is open source. The Barometer, the Calculator, the Analyzer — all live, all free. FinVitals isn't a new idea. It's the thing we've been building toward for years. The crisis just made it urgent.
