01

the problem

Right now, no government agency in the country has a real-time view of their population's financial health. They find out people are struggling after they've already lost housing, skipped medications, and fallen off benefits.

Unemployment data lags by months. Poverty data updates annually. SNAP enrollment numbers get counted after people are already in crisis. By the time a state sees the problem, the damage is done.

And it's about to get worse. H.R. 1 just introduced the largest proposed cuts to SNAP in history, expanded work requirements to age 64, and imposed new Medicaid community engagement requirements. Millions of people are at risk of losing benefits — and most of them don't know it yet.

happening right now

135,000 Kentuckians at risk of losing SNAP. SNAP work requirements expanding to age 64. Medicaid community engagement mandates rolling out state by state. States are scrambling to implement changes they don't have the tools to manage. The people affected are the last to find out.

The programs exist. The funding exists. But governments can't protect people they can't see — and they can't reach people fast enough with the tools they have.

03

how finvitals works

FinVitals adds an AI intelligence layer on top of FinMango's existing infrastructure — turning a dashboard into an early warning system that predicts, identifies, connects, and monitors.

layer one

predict

AI analyzes Barometer signals and identifies which communities are approaching crisis before they hit it. A spike in financial anxiety in Wolfe County plus rising housing stress plus incoming SNAP policy changes doesn't just show up on a dashboard — it triggers an alert. Governments see the problem forming, not after it's arrived.

layer two

identify

Cross-reference population-level signals with eligibility data to identify who specifically is at risk. When H.R. 1 work requirements expand to age 64, FinVitals models which households in which zip codes are affected, what benefits they'll lose, and what alternatives exist. Not "135,000 Kentuckians" — but which ones, where, and what they need.

layer three

connect

Proactively reach affected households with the specific programs they qualify for. This is where the Food Assistance Calculator and Food Desert Analyzer become action tools — triggered by the intelligence layer, not waiting for someone to find them. One conversation, every program, personalized to their situation and location.

layer four

monitor

Track policy changes — H.R. 1, SNAP work requirements, Medicaid shifts, state-level emergency funds — and model their impact on communities in real time. Alert governments and affected households before changes take effect. Nobody gets caught off guard. The Barometer becomes a living policy impact simulator.

04

health, resilience, economy. same problem.

Financial health isn't a category. It's the foundation everything else depends on. When it breaks, health, resilience, and economy break together.

health

56% of young adults report financial stress impacting mental health. When people lose benefits, they skip medications, miss appointments, and end up in emergency rooms. The Barometer already tracks financial anxiety alongside our Google Health partnership published in Nature.

resilience

Communities that can't absorb a financial shock can't recover from anything — policy changes, economic downturns, natural disasters. FinVitals is an early warning system that catches the crisis forming and activates support before communities break.

economy

$140 billion in benefits go unclaimed every year. Every dollar of SNAP benefits returns $1.54 in GDP. FinVitals doesn't just help people — it unlocks economic stimulus that's already funded and sitting on the table.

This is FinMango's thesis: financial health must come before financial education. You can't teach budgeting to someone who can't cover a $500 emergency. You have to stabilize them first. FinVitals is how governments do that at population scale.

05

what governments get

State SNAP and Medicaid directors right now are overwhelmed by H.R. 1 implementation. They have a legal obligation to notify affected households. They don't have the tools to do it effectively. FinVitals gives them three things they've never had:

real-time visibility

See financial stress across your state as it's happening — not six months later in an annual report

predictive intelligence

Know which communities are approaching crisis before they hit it, so you can act instead of react

automatic outreach

Reach affected households with the specific programs they qualify for — without overwhelming caseworker teams

policy impact modeling

See exactly how proposed changes will hit your communities before implementation, not after

Deployment runs through existing infrastructure: state health departments, SNAP offices, 211 centers, public libraries, school districts. Zero new systems to build. FinVitals plugs into what's already there.

07

about FinMango

FinMango is a 501(c)(3) I founded in 2017 with one idea: financial health is a right, not a privilege. What started as a classroom project at Kent State has reached over a million people across 100+ countries.

We do three things: research (with partners like Google Health, WHO, World Bank, and the IMF), education (75,000+ students in Northeast Ohio), and advocacy (a global ambassador network driving policy change). Everything we build is open source. We publish our data, our methodology, and our tools so anyone can use them.

The Financial Health Barometer, the Food Assistance Calculator, the Food Desert Analyzer, five community case studies with the University of Virginia, publications in Nature and the American Journal of Health Education — FinVitals doesn't start from scratch. It's the AI layer that makes everything we've built actually work at the scale the crisis demands.
